Alice in Wonderland

Motion Graphic • Kinetic Typography

Motion Graphic Animation that communicate emotion through a animated mis of text and some images that captures emtion and intonation. This project fouses on how movemnt informs content which is the trailer of “Alice in Wonderland”.

School Project(Group Project)

The project proceeded as a team project. My partner and I picked the theme, audio, concepts, and ideas all together, and I created all the animation by using AfterEffect. We created all the silhouette drawing together. My partner made a second draft for the middle part of the motion graphic (8 sec – 25 sec), and I re-designed all the animation based on my partner's draft. The finalization of this motion graphic were done by me.

Design Decisions

All the inspiration for our motion graphic was from Disney animation, "Alice in Wonderland" (1951). The beginning of our animation starts with a firework in the night sky with stars which is similar to the opening logo scene for Disney movies. The first half of our motion graphic's main colours are sky-blue, white, yellow, and pink. I thought those are the colours that can represent Alice and I followed her outfit and hair colours from the actual Disney animation. The soundtrack we chose had some moments without words. I decided to make a deep hole shape with words to maintain the typography concept. I also wanted to include the scene where Alice falls down into a hole since it is the starting point of her mysterious journey. There were two different styles of animation and design so that I can distinctly show the difference between Alice's real world and the fantasy world after she falls down into the hole. The main theme colour for the other half of the animation (after the hole scene) is black and purple. I included dynamic transitions and smoke effects to make scenes more interesting and fantastical.

StoryBoard

My partner and I brainstormed entire motion graphic again to make it more interesting and clean. Therefore, we had to make new storyboard.

Silhouette for Motion Graphic

These are the silhouette drawings we made for our motion graphic by using photoshop, Illustrator, and procreate. We wanted to include some drawings even though this motion graphic is kinetic typography. Since we also did not want to disturb the typography concept, we decided to make all drawings in silhouette so that it will not make our motion graphic too busy. Most simple drawings were done by me and most complex drawings such as characters in "Alice in Wonderland" were done by my partner.

Challenges/Solution

This was my first rough draft for this project, and there were a lot of problems to fix. At the end of my sound clip, there are short and big sounds every after one sentence. Instead of creating an exciting transition for that sound, we added intense light and changed colour when the sound came out. I thought this idea would make our animation interesting; however, it was not the best decision. Even though the last part of our animation concept is fantasy (wonderland), the change in background colour, and light made my animation too busy and less exciting. I decided to choose only one best colour for the background and create a quick transition effect. Moreover, I made my text layer into three dimensions, but text with no extrusion depth supported us to make more animation with the text and had more smoothness.

What I Learned / Takeaways

I learned and comprehended all 12 principles of animation, and I tried my best to apply all those principles in this project. I think my animation was good but has a few timing issues and towards the end gets a bit too repetitive. I could work on matching the audio rhythm to make my animation more interesting. As for compositions and visual contents, I can put more effort into some overlapping, visual hierarchy alignment, and spacing issues since I have edgy (handwritten/decorative) fonts.
