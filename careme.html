CARE ME

Application Design • UX/UI

CareMe is a healthcare app which strives to easily connect diabetics and seniors with medical professionals. It specializes in featuring services for diabetes. Our aim is to help people take care of their health by providing optimized services and features fordiabetics.

SCHOOL PROJECT (Team Project)

UX / UI Design, Ideation, Storyboard,Mock-up Design, Wireframe, Graphic Design, ProtoPie, UserTesting were my roles. CareMe is specifically designed to cater to the needs of individuals with diabetes. It provides optimized services and features that are tailored to managing diabetes effectively, including blood glucose tracking, medication reminders, meal planning, and personalized health tips related to diabetes.

Goal for CareMe

CareMe is designed to provide an easy way to contact medical professionals and make appointments, as well as to assist seniors who are diabetics in managing their health. With a customizable profile page, the app's streamlined process ensures that individuals with diabetes can quickly and conveniently connect with healthcare providers for consultations, advice, and assistance in managing their condition. Managing health needs can become difficult as we age, especially for conditions like diabetes which can take daily care. People who are diabetic has to take care of their blood-sugar level, food, habits, and regularly have inspections.With the help of this app, users can manage those all at once and the records they have made in CareMe can be a way to check their health condition and history of their health condition.

Features

- Make Appointment: This feature allows users to look up nearby hospitals, previously visited and saved hospitals to easily make an appointment.

- Contact Doctor: This feature allows users to search and easily find a doctor to speak with when they need to. They can make an appointment to call the doctor on an available date.

- Record Glucose: This feature helps users track their glucose levels by automatically recording their levels from their device. They can look up their previous history.

- Diabetic Info: This feature provides useful information to know as a diabetic, such as diet, exercise, and articles to read.

User Research

According to Statistics Canada, 27.9 percent of the elderly live alone, and 48 percent of diabetics are over 65 years of age in Canada. People in this domain are mainly seniors with diabetes. A paper diabetic diary is the easiest way to manage diabetes. However, it is not suitable to look for past records or to compare their blood sugar pattern. People have little idea of how the information they know affects blood sugar. Seniors may have to go to the hospital to see a doctor, but they may not have a car or be able to drive.

UI Storyboard

We created more detailed wireframes, flow-charts, interface storyboards of CareMe's main features to specify and decide how our interface could work and what it could look like. We included details of the style guide and pattern guide elements.

Few Changes After Feedback

We changed red buttons to blue for normal buttons to prevent users from getting confused with a cancel action and changed bottom bar to have a home return feature so users have an easy way to exit their current course of action. We also added a check appointment feature to confirm and show the completed and previous appointments to the user and combined the record glucose function and weekly stats function to allow users to view and record blood glucose graphs at the same time.

Users can check their blood sugar levels by comparing them over a week or hour and record their blood sugar levels and see past records they have recorded

Users can see upcoming appointments and past appointments at a glance and check the location and time of appointments they have made. They can also make another appointment or cancel their appointment.

Usability Testing

We observed six intended users go through a set of tasks provided one by one and interviewed them about the process and interactions of the task after. Identifying any issues users may have that could hinder their ability to navigate the app and any areas that may be confusing or difficult to the user were the main goals for this user testing.

User Interview: We interviewed six users about the process and interactions of the task after to gain more insight into their thoughts behind their actions. We had interviews to identify any issues users may have that could hinder their ability to navigate the app and identify any areas that may be confusing or difficult for the user and identify whether the app meets the intended user’s needs and wants.

Intended Users

From users 1 to 4, we found people who are diabetic to know what they think and who it is helpful to them and people who are seniors or near seniors to see if it is easy for them to use. We also considered if those people have experience in using any type of medical app or not. Since this app is also welcomed by people who are not senior or old, we also wanted to do user testing with young aged people. Users 5 and 6 have done the user testing remotely and they are people who are not diabetic but have the potential to have diabetes in the future to see what they think.

Missing Elements

Problem:It is unable to enlarge the map on an appointment page. If the user doesn’t know the detailed location of the hospital, it will cause confusion in the user experience if it doesn’t work when they try to zoom in on it. Solution is adding zoom in and out on the map, the user will be able to find the location easily.

Problem: There is no enter button to find a hospital and send a message.The lack of an enter button for users to type and send is confusing. Solution is adding an enter button to type and send messages and words

Wrong Design Choice

Proble: Placement of buttons interrupt flow of interaction. Users can become confused if a button is not where they expect it to be. It can cause frustration to the user if they struggle to find what they are looking for. Solution is improving placement of buttons to be in their expected positions in a page.

Problem: The lack of clear wording of the main homepage categories to reflect its actual function.The user can be confused when the labels do not reflect the function and their purpose. It becomes difficult to find what they are looking for. Solution is using proper and clear terminology that will not create any confusion
