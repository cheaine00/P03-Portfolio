BRAND DESIGN GUIDE

Branding

Vorum has been the global leader in orthotics and prosthetics CAD/CAM technology for over 30 years, mainly dedicated to the development of CAD/CAM solutions for the design and fabrication of custom O&P devices.

COOP PROJECT (Collaborative Project)

My Co-op Partner (Graphic Designer) and I were assigned to adjust and develop stronger Vorum's Brand Guide. Design Style Guides help everyone in the business maintain brand consistency. This brand guideline consist of visual elements to help communicate colour usage, brand tone and voice, typography, patterns, and brand principles. It is important to create a cohesive design system for Vorum's own business and brand.

Typeface

These are the typefaces we will be using: Lato, Overpass, and Roboto. Both Lato and Overpass are used as headers and sub-headers- Lato is always in all capitals and bolded heavily, this will often be used for titles. Use Lato sparingly as to not overwhelm any designs as it is a very heavy design element. Overpass will be used in either bold or semi-bold weights, it utilises regular capitalization. Both of these fonts are solid elements in social media advertising, website graphics, product brochures, etc.

Colour & Graphic

Vorum has three key colours: Marine Blue (#174363), Vorum Red (#A30234), and Dark Blue (#0E2130). This is the main colour palette associated with our branding; while some of our material uses an off-white yellow (#FCF8EE) as a background colour. In addition to our main palette, we have also provided a variety of swatches. While our main colour is Vorum Red, it is to be used as an accent colour as to not overwhelm any designs. Instead, Marine Blue is used as our primary colour to contrast the red and bring focus to that red. General direction for graphics goes as follows: clean, bold colours with simple shapes. Colours should be kept flat, and shapes should rarely overlap each other- the only exception being when used behind an image as an additional accent. The idea is to keep our design simple and clean, so as to create a friendlier user experience to replicate our company goals. Parallelograms are used throughout marketing and collateral. Angles can vary depending on the design but should be prominent, parallelograms should also decrease in width the closer it is to a corner. These parallelograms are primarily used in the corner of designs and should be at a lower opacity.

Social Media Graphics

Graphics with photos should include person wearing an O&P device; photos should be edited according to photography standards stated earlier. An overlay may be used over photos if necessary. Unless stated otherwise, photos should have vector shapes and text over it. Standard vector shapes include but are not limited to: abstract lines, polygons, long rectangular shapes, etc. The opacity for vector shapes may be lowered to create harmony with the photo.

Photography

Clinic photos should be portrayed in a clean room with little to no clutter. Product photography should show products and instruments cleanly. Patient Photography should include usage of devices in daily life. Preferably active, outdoor shots. Adding a colour overlay to the picture makes the photo less busy. It can be used as a neat and clean background that can be harmonized with graphic shapes. It also supports the photo to stay within the companyâs colour palette. Challenges/Solution

When it comes to brand design, I only of logo design, editing design, dedicated font development, brand guidelines, and aesthetics as important. I realized it is a false explanation that does not reflect the trend. As the brand experience gradually advanced, the role of brand designers also advanced. Following the design that creates the first brand image, the design that helps the brand grow is also very important and needs profound insight. I learned that the role of a brand designer is not to end with the appearance of a brand that is only visible, but also to the essential work of helping the brand grow.

What I Learned / Takeaways

Brand Styleguide was the project that I started right after I joined the company, so all I did was try to finish the guide as fast as possible so that I can use this guide for future projects . However, I did not understand the company clearly, and I started to create styles that deviate from the design Vorum wanted. I stopped designing and creating style guides and began to study and understand Vorum, products, goals, marketing, and targets intensively. As I fully understood and analyzed the company, it began to be easy to know which design and affinity fit and what should be developed further. In the eight months I worked at Vorum, I completed a lot of projects and made videos, and I was able to better understand and decide what was appropriate and necessary for the brand style.
